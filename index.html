About Me

I started programming at 10 years old in an elementary school computer class, and I've been hooked ever since. The idea that I could create entire experiences with just a keyboard sparked a lifelong love for software development. Growing up in a rural town in south Georgia, I used YouTube tutorials and a shaky internet connection to teach myself how to build websites, games, and basic apps.

In 8th grade, I moved to Coffee County, where I finally had access to formal computer science education. I took CS classes in 8th and 9th grade that taught me proper web development with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Throughout the rest of high school, I shifted focus to engineering classes, drawn to the hands-on nature of building and experimenting. My incredible engineering teacher encouraged me to incorporate computer science into my projects using microcontrollers and online portfolios. It was in these classes that I developed what I consider my most valuable skill: problem solving.

I also joined both the TSA and VEX Robotics teams, competing in video game design contests and building robots to face off in national competitions. I reached the state finals three years in a row, and in my senior year, our team advanced to the World Championship, where I served as the lead programmer. I also served as a Robotics Captain, reporter, vice president, and TSA President during my time in the organization.

After graduating high school, I was accepted into the Georgia Institute of Technology, where I'm currently pursuing a degree in Computer Science. At Georgia Tech, I've had the opportunity to explore everything from full-stack development to human-computer interaction, while collaborating on real-world projects with teams across campus. Outside the classroom, I became heavily involved in my fraternity, Chi Psi, where I've served as both Lodge Manager and Tech Chair. In these roles, I was responsible for managing daily operations of the fraternity's Lodge, implementing digital systems to streamline our workflows, and ensuring the space remained organized, functional, and tech-forward for all our members.

Being a part of Chi Psi has also given me the chance to give back to the community in meaningful ways. Through our partnership with Trees Atlanta, I've volunteered to help remove invasive plant species and restore local green spaces across the city. Each year, we also organize a large-scale fundraiser for the Atlanta Community Food Bank, an event that brings together students, alumni, and neighbors to raise thousands of dollars for families facing food insecurity. These experiences have deepened my appreciation for service and the impact small, consistent efforts can have on the people around us.

Education

My coursework includes:

Systems and Architecture – Gained insight into how computers operate at a physical level, working with low-level programming languages and understanding how high-level code compiles into machine instructions.

I also completed a two-semester Junior Design Capstone Project in collaboration with an M.D. at Emory University Hospital, where our team planned, prototyped, and developed a medication information and dosage conversion website. The project aimed to integrate fragmented tools into a single, intuitive platform to help healthcare professionals make faster and safer clinical decisions.

These experiences have strengthened not only my technical skills, but also my ability to work across disciplines, communicate with clients, and iterate quickly on feedback.
